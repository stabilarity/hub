# Georgia Tax Reform & Shadow Economy Analysis

Research data and charts for "Georgia's Tax Reform Miracle — Flat Tax Impact on Shadow Economy"

## Data Sources
- World Economics (Informal Economy estimates)
- World Bank (Tax Revenue % GDP)
- IMF Country Reports
- Torosyan (2014) - Tax Reform in Georgia and the Size of the Shadow Economy
- Schneider (2016) - Shadow Economies of 157 Countries

1. **chart1_shadow_economy_timeline.png** - Shadow Economy 2000-2025 with reform markers

3. **chart3_regional_comparison.png** - Georgia vs regional peers (2024)
4. **chart4_dual_correlation.png** - Shadow Economy vs Tax Revenue dual axis

## Key Findings
- Shadow economy reduced from 68.8% (2000) to 41.5% (2024) = 27.3 percentage points
- Tax revenue increased from 13.5% (2000) to 24.56% (2024) = 11 percentage points
- Flat tax reform (2005) marked inflection point
